## 📋 About the application

AOMEI Partition Assistant manages your computer storage devices. This tool allows you to perform complex disk operations through a simple interface. You can resize, merge, and split partitions on your hard drive or solid-state drive. The software includes features to migrate your operating system to new storage hardware, convert disk styles between MBR and GPT, and recover lost partitions. These tasks occur without data loss when you follow the prompts.

## 🛠 Features

*   **Resize Partitions:** Adjust the size of your partitions to create more space for files or applications.
*   **Merge Partitions:** Combine two adjacent partitions into one large partition.
*   **Split Partitions:** Divide one large partition into two separate segments to better organize your data.
*   **OS Migration:** Move your Windows installation to an SSD for faster performance.
*   **MBR to GPT Conversion:** Convert your disk format to meet modern motherboard requirements without deleting your data.
*   **Disk Cloning:** Create an exact copy of your hard drive for backups or hardware upgrades.
*   **Data Protection:** Use built-in patterns to prevent data loss during common partitioning operations.
*   **Partition Recovery:** Search for and restore partitions deleted by mistake.

## 💡 How to resize a partition

Resizing a partition involves changing the amount of space allocated to a specific drive letter. 

1. Launch the AOMEI Partition Assistant application.
2. Select the disk or partition you wish to modify from the primary menu.
3. Choose the "Resize/Move Partition" option from the sidebar or right-click menu.
4. Drag the slider to the left or right to set your new size.
5. Click "OK" to apply the setting.
6. Click the "Apply" button at the top-left of the main interface to execute the change.
7. Confirm your choice if the software prompts you for a final check.

## 🔄 Moving your operating system to an SSD

Moving your OS improves system speed significantly. 

1. Connect your new SSD to your computer.
2. Open AOMEI Partition Assistant.
3. Select "Migrate OS to SSD/HDD" from the "Wizards" menu on the left.
4. Select your destination SSD as the target drive.
5. Review the partitions created on the new drive.
6. Click "Next" and then "Finish".
7. Click "Apply" in the top corner to start the migration process.
8. Restart your computer when the software finishes.

## 🛡 Maintaining data safety

Disk management carries inherent risks. Always follow these guidelines to keep your files safe:

*   **Backup:** Back up your important files to an external drive or cloud storage before you modify partition sizes.
*   **Power:** Keep your computer connected to a stable power source. If you use a laptop, ensure the battery has a full charge.
*   **Interruption:** Do not turn off, restart, or disconnect power while the software performs a task.
*   **Health:** Check your hard drive for errors before performing large operations like moving an OS. Use the built-in Windows Check Disk tool if you suspect hardware issues.

## 🧐 Technical details for administrators

The software functions by interacting directly with the partition table of your storage devices. It bypasses temporary obstacles by creating virtual queues for operations. When you click "Apply," the software creates a schedule for the disk change. If an operation requires a restart, the software will create a boot-level instruction to complete the task before Windows fully loads. This prevents conflicts with files already in use by the system.

## ❓ Troubleshooting common issues

If you encounter a problem, check these common solutions:

*   **Application does not open:** Ensure you have administrator rights on your Windows account. Right-click the icon and choose "Run as administrator."
*   **Drive is locked:** Some drives contain files currently in use. The software will ask to restart the computer to finish the task. Allow this restart to occur.
*   **Insufficient space:** Ensure your target drive has enough room for the data you plan to move. If you lack space, delete unnecessary files before you begin.
*   **Not showing drives:** Check your drive cables and connections if a disk does not appear in the menu. Also, ensure the disk does not have severe physical damage.
